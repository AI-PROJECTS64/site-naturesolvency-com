NatureSolvency.com — Concept Note

This note outlines a possible positioning for NatureSolvency.com as a descriptive, neutral digital asset dedicated to nature-related financial risks and what we call “nature solvency”. It is not a rating methodology, not a policy document and not a legal or investment opinion.

1. Idea: from nature-related risks to “nature solvency”

Over the last few years, initiatives such as TNFD have helped financial institutions and corporates acknowledge that:

  • a large share of economic activity depends on ecosystem services (water regulation, soil fertility, pollination, coastal protection, etc.),
  • the degradation of nature can translate into credit, market and operational risks,
  • nature loss can ultimately become a solvency question for certain business models, sectors or territories.

Nature solvency is a way of framing this: how resilient are cash flows, assets and liabilities when ecosystem services are stressed, degraded or lost? NatureSolvency.com is proposed as a neutral banner for that question.

2. Strategic context (2025–2035)

Between 2025 and 2035, several trends are likely to shape the space:

  • More mandatory disclosures on nature-related risks and dependencies,
  • integration of nature into prudential frameworks, stress tests and risk appetites,
  • emergence of data platforms linking geospatial, ecological and financial information,
  • pressure from supervisors, NGOs and investors to move beyond climate-only metrics.

In that landscape, NatureSolvency.com can act as a long-lived label for frameworks, observatories, indices or platforms that connect biodiversity, ecosystems and solvency in a language understandable by boards, risk committees and regulators.

3. Potential acquirers & positioning

The domain is best suited to actors who need a neutral, non-product-specific banner:

  • banks, insurers and large asset owners building nature-risk strategies,
  • stock exchanges, index providers or data platforms working on nature-related benchmarks,
  • multilateral, public–private or philanthropic initiatives on nature and finance,
  • specialised climate–nature platforms expanding into solvency and prudential topics.

The positioning should remain clearly descriptive: NatureSolvency.com is the banner, not the guarantee. The credibility comes from the governance and methodologies deployed behind it, not from the name alone.

4. Illustrative use-cases (within clear boundaries)

4.1 Possible directions

  • “Nature Solvency Framework” describing how nature-related risks feed into capital, provisions and business model resilience.
  • Nature Solvency Observatory monitoring sectoral or regional exposures, without providing individual issuer credit opinions.
  • Scenario & training hub for nature-related stress tests, supporting prudential and internal risk work.
  • Knowledge platform aggregating methodologies, case studies and supervisory expectations on nature-related solvency risks.

4.2 Non-target uses

  • Presenting NatureSolvency.com as a regulated credit rating agency or official supervisory body (unless such status is formally obtained),
  • marketing it as a guarantee of solvency or as an automatic “seal of safety” for specific products,
  • using the brand to give the impression of regulatory approval where none exists.

5. Legal, regulatory and reputational framing

Any initiative under NatureSolvency.com will have to coexist with:

  • prudential regulation for banks and insurers,
  • securities, disclosure and listing rules,
  • ESG and sustainability reporting requirements,
  • nature-related regulations at national and regional level.

The domain name itself:

  • does not confer any regulatory status or licence,
  • does not replace formal credit ratings, internal models or supervisory assessments,
  • is a communication and framing asset, which can support serious work but cannot substitute it.

This document does not constitute legal, financial, supervisory or investment advice. It is an indicative, non-binding articulation of potential uses for the domain.

6. Relation to the broader “Solvency” family

In some strategies, NatureSolvency.com could be combined with other thematic assets such as WaterSolvency.com, EnergySolvency.com and ClimateSolvency.com to build a broader “Global Solvency Framework”.

The idea is not to merge all risks into a single score, but to provide a coherent vocabulary for boards and supervisors when discussing the solvency impact of:

  • water stress and hydrological risks,
  • energy transition and energy-system shocks,
  • climate-related physical and transition risks,
  • nature and biodiversity loss.

NatureSolvency.com is the nature pillar in that narrative: focused on ecosystems and biodiversity, not competing with existing climate-only frameworks but complementing them.

7. Transaction & contact

A potential transaction for NatureSolvency.com would typically follow:

  • NDA and initial discussions about intended use, governance and safeguards,
  • formal offer and agreement on price and transfer conditions,
  • escrow process and transfer of the NatureSolvency.com domain name.

Only the domain name is transferred. No data, models, software, indices, methodologies, teams or advisory services are included by default.

All future uses remain the sole responsibility of the acquirer and its governance, risk and compliance structures.
